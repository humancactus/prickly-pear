One time I went to Chiptole and just watched the line for an hour. Chiptole served 150 people that hour! Each person behind the counter was probably paid about a burrito for that hour (yes I'm aware this is unusual behavior). The two parts I think are potentially interesting are separating the supply chain from the menu, and then dynamically pricing to optimize inventory distribution of demand. By that I mean 1) designing a restaurant in such a way that shopping for ingredients isn't driven by the menu. The menu evolves flexibly based on what is purchsd, so purchasing decisions can optimize for price and quality of food.
